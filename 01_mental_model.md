# Wave — A Mental Model

This document is not marketing copy and not documentation.
It is orientation.

Its purpose is to describe how Wave should be understood, not how it should be used.
This page exists to prevent Wave from becoming shallow as it grows.

Wave is a place where things move.

People come to Wave to speak, coordinate, build, argue, document, joke, plan, and sometimes just be present. What they do — and how seriously they take it — is up to them. Wave doesn’t enforce a culture. It provides a structure that cultures can grow inside.

To understand Wave, it helps to understand a few simple ideas.

## Waves

A wave is any unit of expression.

A wave can be a thought, a message, a task, a proposal, a document, a reaction, or a conversation. Some waves are small and pass quickly. Others grow, branch, and settle into something long-lasting.

Wave doesn’t decide which waves matter. Communities do.

What Wave does is make sure waves:
- can move
- can interact
- can be revisited
- can become stable when needed

Nothing is forced to disappear just because it’s old.
Nothing is forced to stay important just because it was loud.

## Spaces

A space is a bounded place.

Spaces are where waves live. Each space has its own members, roles, norms, and rhythm. Some spaces are playful. Some are serious. Some are chaotic. Some are tightly run.

Wave doesn’t assume how a space should behave.

A space can be:
- a public community
- a private team
- a project
- an experiment
- a long-running archive

Spaces are isolated by design, but not sealed off. When communities choose to, waves can reach beyond their original space. Users can be part of multiple places at the same time and they share relationship and references while doing so.

## Channels

A channel is what’s happening now.

Wave supports real-time presence, live updates, and collaboration, but it doesn’t reduce everything to a feed. Channels exist so people can feel where activity is flowing without being forced to chase it.

You can enter a channel.
You can leave it.
You can come back later and still understand what happened.

## Settling

Not all waves are meant to keep moving.

Some waves settle.

When something matters — a decision, a guide, a plan, shared knowledge — Wave allows it to stabilize. Settled waves become references: documents, wikis, pinned context, or structured work.

Settling is not a reward.
It’s not a promotion.
It’s a choice made by people who need continuity.

## Structure without prescription

Wave combines execution.

Waves can turn into:
- conversations
- governance polls
- boards
- tasks
- assignments
- workflows
- long-lived artifacts

But Wave never assumes that they must.

Some communities will use Wave like a forum.
Others like a chat room.
Others like a project management system.
Many will mix or alternate.

Wave supports this by being explicit about mechanics, and neutral about meaning.

## Responsibility

Wave assumes authorship.

What you create is attached to you.
What you start can travel.
What you say can reach farther than you expect.

Wave doesn’t optimize for virality or noise. It optimizes for clarity, persistence, and choice. How much impact something has is shaped by the people who engage with it, not by hidden incentives.

## What Wave is not

Wave is not a feed.
Wave is not an algorithm.
Wave is not a single way of communicating.

It’s a system that allows different ways of communicating to coexist — without collapsing into chaos or freezing into rules.

## In short

Wave gives structure to motion.

What you do with it — casually or seriously, briefly or over years — is up to you and the spaces you’re part of.

## End note

Wave is built on infrastructure designed to be secure, durable, and predictable — so that communities don’t have to think about it. The complexity stays underneath. What surfaces is simply a place where things can move and settle naturally.
