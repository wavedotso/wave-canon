# Wave — Limits

This document defines what Wave intentionally does **not** do.

These limits are not temporary constraints or missing features. They are structural decisions designed to preserve Wave’s ability to host many kinds of communities without collapsing into noise, coercion, or hidden power.

If a future change conflicts with the limits described here, the change is wrong.

---

## No Algorithmic Amplification

Wave does not algorithmically amplify content.

There is no system whose purpose is to maximize attention, engagement, or time spent. Waves are not promoted because they provoke reactions, spread quickly, or fit a predicted profile.

Ordering, visibility, and reach are shaped by:
- time
- space boundaries
- explicit references
- deliberate human action

This ensures that meaning emerges from participation, not optimization.

---

## No Pay-to-Speak Dynamics

Money never increases a person’s ability to speak, post, or be heard.

Support mechanisms increase the **capacity of spaces**, not the influence of individuals. A user with no paid plan has the same expressive rights as one with significant support history.

Wave does not sell:
- reach
- priority
- visibility
- authority

If money affects voice, Wave has failed.

---

## No Hidden Incentives

Wave avoids incentives that distort behavior invisibly.

There are no opaque scoring systems, shadow rankings, or engagement-based rewards. When something changes how Wave behaves, that change is explicit and understandable.

Users should never need to guess:
- why something surfaced
- why something faded
- why something mattered

Opacity creates manipulation. Wave rejects it.

---

## No Universal Culture

Wave does not enforce a single culture, tone, or mode of participation.

There is no global standard for seriousness, humor, productivity, or civility. Spaces define their own norms, and Wave provides boundaries that allow those norms to coexist.

Global rules exist only to protect the system itself, not to shape community identity.

---

## No Forced Ephemerality

Wave does not assume that everything should disappear.

Content may fade in relevance, but it is not erased by default. Communities choose what to archive, what to stabilize, and what to let pass.

History is a feature, not a liability.

---

## No Infinite Growth Requirement

Wave is not optimized for endless expansion.

Small, quiet, long-lived spaces are first-class citizens. A space does not need to grow to justify its existence, and success is not measured by scale alone.

Wave supports depth as much as breadth.

---

## No Ownership Without Accountability

Wave does not allow anonymous control over shared spaces.

Authority — where it exists — is explicit, visible, and revocable. Governance mechanisms favor clarity over convenience.

Power without accountability erodes trust. Wave treats trust as infrastructure.

---

## In Short

Wave is deliberately limited.

These limits exist so that Wave can remain a place where:
- expression is free
- structure is reliable
- support does not corrupt meaning

The absence of certain features is what makes everything else possible.
